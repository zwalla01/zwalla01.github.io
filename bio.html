About Me

Hi, I'm Zachary Wallace, a senior at Tufts University majoring in applied mathematics and minoring in computer science. I'm from La Jolla California and grew up there all my life.

One important thing that you should know about me first is that I am a very competitive swimmer for Tufts University Swimming and Diving. We compete in the NCAA Division 3 and are part of the New England Small College Athletic Conference (NESCAC). As a swimmer, I am a 6 time All-NESCAC team member (top 3 in the conference per event) and a 5 time NCAA All-American (top 16 nationally per event). I am also a multi-event record holder for the swim team and a one-time NESCAC performer of the week. Swimming has always been extremely important to my life as well as an important aspect of my college career.

Aside from athletics, academically I am very passionate about math and computer science. I am very fascinated by the overlap between math and computer science and ways that overlap can be used to analyze and interpret extremely large sets of data. All in all, I'm very curious and fascinated by the field big-data, and am especially interested in the analysis of large sets of medical data, which is why I am also really interested in Bioinformatics.

I also have a lot of hobbies. Having grown up in La Jolla, I absolutley love the ocean and everything about it. I love to fish off our Riviera fishing boat with my dad, scuba dive, spear fish, surf, and compete in surf lifesaving competitions. I am also extremely passionate about comic books and other associated popular arts like Marvel and DC television shows and movies. I consider myself an avid comic book reader and collector and have attended the San Diego Comic Con over the past few years.
